A little story about the deveopment of this project

The Idea

I wanted to code something like this for very long. I really liked the idea of it; not too difficult to implement, a lot of fun to play around with and maybe even fun for ethnologists.

However, when i saw the first video of some guy on youtube visualizing the algorithms in a very similar fashion, i did not only have no idea about the algorithms used, I also didn't know nuthin about programming something like this.

Of course, i wanted my program, if i ever built something like it, to look nice, otherwise it wouldn't be as much fun - swing was out of question.

Algorithms 1 - studying computer science

Then, I started studying computer science. Already in the second semester I heard Algorithms 1 and read the book "Algorithms and data structures" (i think by Niklaus Wirth). I really liked what i learned in this course and started to implement the algorithms i came across in java. At this point the only interaction was thtough the command line.

Algorithms 1 - my own tutorium

Fast forward two semesters and we are in the present. I am holding a Tutorium for Algorithms 1 and having a lot of fun doing it.

A Tutorium is a class, for students to attend. For algorithms 1, they first hear the lecture about the subject from the professor and then a small group of students (20-30 per Tutorium) come together and i help them understand the subject, as well as giving them excersices, grading their 'homework'...

It is in this class, that i revisit all the algorithms, i learned two semesters ago. My understanding of them is now deepend. One day i am preparing a lecture about Heaps. Of course, i wanted to cover Heapsort in this tutorium, as it is only a logical product of buildHeap() and heapify(). When visiting the wikipedia web-page of heapsort , you can see a little animation of it. Thats what finally triggered the development of this website:
